Interpreter

The (460 chars) quantum interpreter is based on references arXiv:1711.02086 and arXiv:1608.03355. For simplicity, we always measure at the end of the execution:

Shor's algorithm

As a test case, we employ the quantum circuit of Shor's algorithm for the number fifteen and base eleven, following references arXiv:1804.03719 and arXiv:2306.09122. The resulting compiled circuit uses five qubits, three of which serve as control. To enhance statistical accuracy, the experiment is repeated multiple times. Additionally, we define a classical post-processing function: 

n‿a‿qb‿r ← ⟨15, 11, 5, 0 U˜ 2⋆3⟩

Epilogue

Why BQN, you may ask? Its tacit and functional style together with its support for numerous combinators, which makes programming feel like solving a fun algebraic problem rather than drafting a manifesto. Try running the simulation in the BQN repl and explore it! While the interpreter is not particularly optimized, here is a comparison with Common Lisp for those who are curious:
